He played his first gig at his own birthday party held at the Tabassco Club and the response was overwhelming even though the band only knew fourteen songs. The experience was so gratifying it inspired him to go further to bring his dream into a reality ... to seek engagements and to eventually record!

He did just that under his own independent label Thai's Who We Be Records which was also the title of his first CD That's Who We Be released in 1998. In the year 2000 he released his second CD entitled Bringin The Dream and his newly released CD is entitled Bringin The Hear which reinforces his goal that Zydeco is a musical force to be reckoned with!

Nooney's creative musical blend of licks and lyrics have thought provoking purpose ... to make Zydeco an aspiring genre to be resilient, recognized, and respected He wants that acknowledgement for what he calls old school Zydeco by the greats such as the late Beau Jacque and Rocking Sidney but mainly for its genesis to date that he helped to create under the mentorship of Jeffrey Broussard and Step Rideau!

Nooney's blend of R&B, with a slash of soul and a dash of Creole/Cajun seasoning combined with inventive choreographed steps implode to phenomenal heights of originality for a new wave Zydeco ... Nooney style! It's Zydeco zeal in a highly energetic show that always ignites a crowd and these accordion architects of sound can build monumental excitement!

Nooney had no idea ... then... but today this courageous young man is on a mission and he has plenty of ideas! Just take a listen 'cause he is and will always be bringing the heat!

Frank E. Young Jr. (Nooney)

Frank E. Young Jr. had no idea that one day when he embarked on a trail ride for fun that he would have an epiphany ... to learn to play the accordion! Listening to the group Zydeco Force during that trail ride,he became so engulfedin Zydeco music that his passion to play resulted in him being transformed

several years later into the man in demand to bring audiences to their feet as ... Nooney and the Zydeco Floaters!

Affectionately nick named "Nooney" as a toddler by his mother, this Houston native grew up in an area known as Fidelity with his greatest wealth being strong community ties. A natural athlete due to his father Frank E. Young Sr. being a renowned Welterweight champion, Nooney participated in all sports while at Galena Park High and really had no interest in music other than listening to it. During those years he jammed to a little R&B and rap with Maze, The Isley Brothers, LL Cool J and Ice Cube topping as his favorite artists.
