In 1998 I, along with a friend started a company that was going to be a web based company.  The name of the company was ISP International, ISP an acronym for International Study Program.  The company was based upon the need that Japanese students had for homestay families while attending College in the United States.  My friend had moved to Japan to be in charge of setting up an office for processing new applicants and a sort of escort for travel arrangements.  I on the other hand was to be head of the US office and in charge of several areas, including the following;

 

  1. Homestay Family Recruitment
  2. English School (for students that wanted private lessons rather than typical college ESL)
  3. Tour Guide Planning and Safety
  4. Web Based Marketing for International Promotion

 

Initial Web Planning

 At the time the internet was still at the peak of profitability and the best place to stir up international interest.  I along with the help of my partner began the initial planning and design of the web site www.ispint.com.  It was to be used to mainly promote homestay packages for international students who wanted to come to America for college or to learn English.  Secondary the site was to be used to collect and recruit host families that lived in the Utah area.

 

Web Marketing Process

We began development of the site with a design that would only include promotion and basically a free place for people to come and meet others.  With careful design of content and Meta Tag use we began to submit the site to major search engines through several techniques and different types of software, Web Position Gold being one of those applications.  Funds were set aside for advertising and submission to the major search engines including Yahoo and Excite (at the time Excite was still a major search engine site).  In addition we began to seek out English related sites and began to promote through the use of Yahoo clubs (now groups.yahoo.com).  We were listed fairly fast with several of the search engines and linked by several major English learning websites.

The main point of our now growing free site was a type of pen pal page for people from all over the world to come and post messages to meet new people from other countries (mostly English and Japanese Speaking, since the site was in Japanese and English).  As a side promotion we used this new found popularity to show off the major announcement of a Homestay program “coming soon.”

We promoted in this style for approximately 3 months, during which time we collected names and e-mail contacts under a statistical and logistical plan, such as age groups, location and the interest to learn English.  After three months we had tens of thousands of posts and traffic of approximately 10,000 unique hits per day.

Candidates that fit a specific detail were e-mailed and promoted too further urging them to participate in a trial period homestay in which they only had to pay for travel expense. 

 

“Imagine learning English and experiencing how Americans live by staying with a homestay family in the United States.  All for the price of your Airfare alone!”

 

This promotion was very successful and brought us several thousand candidates that filled out online applications for intent to participate.  Also we found several Host Families willing to host students for different extended periods of time.

 

Continued Development and Marketing

The initial group that came was very difficult and took a lot of investment and trial by error, but overall it was very successful.  Students were escorted to the Airport by my partner in Japan and greeted by me, a Japanese speaking employee, and their respective host families in the US.  For a month we tested what was needed and how to proceed with future clients.

After the trial period we were very confident in how to run our operation and began to process the waiting list that had developed over the months of promotion.  We were now listed as high as 3rd on all the major search engines for keywords such as “English” “Homestay” “Hosting” “English Schools” and a wide variety of keywords related to our business.

In Addition we now implemented our Customer satisfaction promotion with document and video taped sessions of our satisfied clients during the trial period.

 

ISP production

Over a Three year period ISP international grew with hard work and personal investment.  We were now promoting Colleges and even vacation/tour packages for Japanese students as well as several other students in many different countries.  We had a waiting list that was very large and almost overwhelming.  I had hired up to 5 additional consultants and translators during my time with the business and worked just as hard with continued marketing on the website as well as person to person promotion with host families which I chose to personally interview before allowing them to host one of my clients.

Over three years I maintained the business and website doing everything from teaching English to escorting students to places such as Disneyland and the Grand Canyon.

 

Fate of ISP

After nearly three years of working hard everyday with my new found success I was offered a buy out by an English school in Japan, who wanted to run the business for themselves.  After much consideration I took their offer and turned the entire business over to them.  This for me was something that I had to do since I must never have had a break in the 3 years I ran the company and my desire to do something different.

I decided to return to School moving to San Diego in 2001.  ISP International has since been dissolved due to what I believe is a lack of commitment on the US side of the new business.  Ispint.com is no longer online, but was reorganized to some point at www.oeschool.com which now solely based in Japan.

If you do a search on Google.com for ispint.com you can still see some of the remaining links that are still present from a company that died out 2 ½ years ago.

 

Epilogue

It was sad for me to see all of my hard work disappear after I chose to turn it over to another organization, but at the same time I am very relieved that I have time now to do things that I have always wanted to do.  One of the most precious things that came out of the experience is that I was able to meet my wife.  She had posted messages on my site and later I hired her as an employee to do Japanese translation.  It is my hope that I can take this experience and use it in future projects that I have plans for related to Web Marketing and business promotion.
